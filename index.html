About

🎮

Welcome to AHS Game Library!

AHS Game Library is a collection of educational games created by teachers from Rainbow Centre Admiral Hill School. These resources support learning across four key domains: Literacy, Numeracy, Social Emotional Learning, and Daily Living.

Our goal is to make games accessible for students with different abilities, and provide a platform for them to learn, explore, and succeed at their own pace.

We also hope to support families by providing resources that parents and caregivers can easily use at home to practise and reinforce important skills.

📐

Game Design Guidelines

Games in the AHS Game Library are designed with students with autism in mind. The following principles are used to guide our design process toward creating accessible, engaging, and effective learning tools.

🗂️ Structure

Students with autism thrive with predictability. A well-structured game removes uncertainty — students always know what to do, how much is left, and when they're done. This clarity reduces anxiety and keeps students focused on learning rather than figuring out what's expected of them.

🎨 Sensory Management

Sensory overload can quickly derail a learning experience. Calm visuals, gentle audio, a pressure-free pace, and the flexibility to control these settings create an environment where students feel safe and comfortable — making it far easier for them to engage, concentrate, and enjoy the game.

♿ Accessibility & Differentiation

No two students are the same. Games should meet students where they are — supporting different abilities, preferences and learning paces. When a game is designed for every student, every student gets a genuine chance to succeed.
